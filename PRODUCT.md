# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

General public — people curious about migration data who may not have a policy background. They arrive from social media, news links, or direct search. They want to understand the scale and human dimension of migration, not just numbers.

## Product Purpose

A data journalism visualization that makes US migration encounter data tangible and human. The particle map grabs attention with kinetic energy (dots swarming the country), while the editorial sections reframe the data around real people who contribute to society. Success: a visitor leaves understanding both the scale and the humanity behind the numbers.

## Brand Personality

Precise, kinetic, humanizing.

Data-focused but never cold. The interface earns trust through accuracy and clarity, then uses motion and visual storytelling to make the data feel alive. Flashy where it serves comprehension — restrained where it doesn't.

## Anti-references

- Cluttered news dashboards with too many widgets
- Sterile government .gov data portals
- Generic SaaS metric dashboards
- Overly academic or dry statistical presentations
- Gratuitous infographics that prioritize decoration over data

## Design Principles

1. **Data is people** — every visualization choice should reinforce that these numbers represent human beings with lives, families, and contributions.
2. **Earn attention, then inform** — the particle map and kinetic elements hook the visitor; the editorial sections deliver substance.
3. **Precision builds trust** — sourced data, clear methodology, no sensationalism.
4. **Show scale through motion** — static charts flatten magnitude; animation reveals it.
5. **Sister to Track Policy** — shares visual DNA with track-policy (same design system, typography, color language) while focusing on migration data specifically.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum
- Respect prefers-reduced-motion (already implemented)
- Ensure particle animations don't trigger vestibular issues at default settings
- Screen reader accessible data tables as fallback for visualizations
